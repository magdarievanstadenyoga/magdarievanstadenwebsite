There is information everywhere around us, and within us, if we only choose to see it.

A felt pull toward something. The quiet unease that surfaces and gets buried again. You catch glimpses of this version of you in the things that light you up, and then you lose that again.

These moments are not random. They are signals. Guiding you, helping you find your way. And most of us have simply stopped being still enough, quiet enough, present enough to listen to them.

The Polynesian navigators of the Pacific, the original wayfinders, crossed thousands of miles of open ocean without instruments. No maps, no compass. They read the stars, they felt the swell and the currents beneath the hull, observed the flight of birds, the colour of the water. They trusted what they could feel.

They knew exactly where they were going because they had learned to pay attention to what was already moving around them, and within them. This retreat is named for that practice.

“I don't know where I am going, but I know exactly how to get there.”Renias — from The Lion Tracker's Guide to Life by Boyd Varty
About the retreat

A weekend of paying attention.

Wayfinding is a weekend retreat on the KZN South Coast built around cultivating the art of paying attention to what's actually moving in you, beneath the hum of everything urgent. We'll move through yoga, qigong, traditional yogic breathwork, restorative practices, somatic movement, sound healing, meditation and journaling as our core set of tools to help you tune back in to that.

At its core, this retreat is about your nervous system — shifting out of stress and constant doing into a deep, dynamic rest that genuinely repairs and restores.

This retreat is not miraculously going to fix everything and give you all the answers. But it can be the beginning of the journey that returns you to yourself, the part that already knows, living quietly beneath the noise of ordinary life.

The practice

Move, breathe, and finally be still.

Strong morning practice and slow restorative evenings, in a glass studio set in the forest — with the occasional duiker wandering past.

Is this for you?

Wherever you are, you're welcome here.

Maybe you've been functioning well on the outside but running on something closer to empty on the inside. You can't remember the last time you were truly quiet. You've been meaning to slow down and keep not doing it.

Maybe you practice yoga regularly — in a studio, at the gym or online at home — and you've always been curious about what it feels like to go deeper than a single hour allows. A retreat is where the practice lands differently: more time, more space, more stillness to work with.

Or maybe you've been on retreat before and you know exactly what you're coming back for.

You don't need to be an experienced yogi. You don't need to have it figured out. You don't need to be perfect. You just need to be willing to show up.

A sense of the days

Unhurried, by design.

Friday afternoon, arrive, put your bags down, and step out of your default world and into this one — our retreat container for the weekend. From this point there is genuinely nothing you need to do, nowhere you need to be, nothing waiting on you. We'll begin gently: a restorative practice, a shared meal, a fire ceremony to set intention.

Saturday begins at sunrise — that quiet window just before the world stirs — with qigong, breathwork, meditation and journaling. Then yoga, brunch, perhaps the beach. The afternoon moves into deeper work, followed by yin yoga, dinner and a non-sleep-deep-rest practice of yoga nidra.

Sunday is slower and softer by design. A full practice, a sound bath, brunch shared together. We close in a loving-kindness (metta) meditation. You check out at 2pm — perhaps feeling a bit different to when you arrived…

About Pumula Retreat

Pumula means "to rest."

An easy one-hour drive from Durban along the South Coast, you'll find Pumula Retreat Centre, tucked into the tropical Natal forests. Pumula means "to rest" in Zulu — and the space most certainly invites you to drop into that.

We'll be staying on the property with its lush forest gardens, either in the main house or the cottage just steps away, with rooms offering either an ocean view or forest view — each one worth waking up for. Our practice will be in the glass yoga studio with the occasional duiker wandering past.

Lucie and Scott, the owners, are passionate about food. You will be fed well: wholesome, plant-based meals that show you healthy food can be delicious at the same time. During your free time there is opportunity to head to the beach or simply relax by the pool, in a hammock or curled up with a book from their well-stocked yoga library. A massage or treatment is also available at your own cost, and is highly recommended as part of your time here.

About Mags

Your guide for the weekend.

Magdarie van Staden (Mags) is a 500-hour trauma-informed yoga teacher with over ten years' experience of teaching yoga and hosting retreats. She draws from hatha, vinyasa, yin, somatic movement and qigong to move the body the way it's meant to move — connected to breath, anchored in sensation, becoming still.

In practice the focus is always the same: come back to a felt sense in the body. Sessions move between slower nervous system resets and stronger, heat-building practices. Both are equally needed.

In a retreat, Mags holds a container for you to step away from default mode — the constant doing, the mental noise, the forgetting. To give your nervous system a real exhale.

If you have never been on retreat before, it is one of the most precious things you can do for yourself. Come and see.

Good to know

Frequently asked.

Is this suitable for beginners?

Absolutely. This retreat is open to all levels. If you've never done yoga before, this is actually a beautiful place to start — modifications and support are built into every session. More experienced? No problem. You'll be offered various levels and progressions to move deeper.

Can I come alone?

Yes — and many people do. It's one of the things about retreats: you arrive not knowing anyone and leave with people who feel like they've known you for years.

Can I book a shared room if I'm coming alone?

Definitely. We'll pair you with someone else of the same gender who is also in a shared room.

Do I need my own yoga mat?

No. Mats and all supports are provided. You're welcome to bring your own if you prefer.

What about special dietary requirements?

Please indicate special dietary requirements on your registration form and Lucie and Scott at Pumula will take care of you. Kindly note certain requirements may incur a surcharge between R100–R150 (e.g. gluten-free +R100).

Is there wifi?

Yes — but we'd gently encourage you to use it mindfully. A retreat offers a perfect opportunity to disconnect and unplug.

What if I need to cancel?

We know life can be uncertain. Our full cancellation policy is set out above — in short, the R1,000 deposit is non-refundable, and the refund on the balance depends on how far in advance you let us know in writing.
