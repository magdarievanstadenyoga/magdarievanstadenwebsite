Balances the nervous system. A great place to start.

How to do Kapalabhati Sit tall with spine erect. Exhale sharply through the nose — belly draws in and up with each pump. The inhale is passive, simply let the belly release. Keep the pace steady and rhythmic.

After completing your pumps: exhale fully and hold out (bahya kumbhaka) — apply all three bandhas (mula, uddiyana, jalandhara). Then inhale fully and hold in (antara kumbhaka) — mula + jalandhara only.

When to avoid
Avoid during pregnancy, menstruation, high blood pressure, heart conditions, or epilepsy. Not recommended if you have a hernia, glaucoma, recent abdominal surgery, or on a full stomach. Stop and rest if you feel dizzy, nauseous, or lightheaded.

When to avoid
Kumbhaka (breath retention) is not advised during pregnancy. If you have a respiratory condition, start with shorter holds or omit the retention entirely. Never force or strain — the breath should feel expansive, not effortful.
