Why it matters

Burnout is quietly expensive.

Chronic stress doesn't stay at the desk. It shows up as cortisol dysregulation, short fuses, foggy decisions and people who are physically present but quietly checked out. Regulating the nervous system isn't a perk — it's infrastructure for how your team thinks, decides and works together.

This is nervous-system work, not a stretch break.

Most corporate wellness stops at surface level — a smoothie bar, a step challenge, a one-off talk that fades by Friday. My work goes a layer deeper: into the body, the breath, and the autonomic nervous system that quietly governs how regulated, resilient and connected your people actually are.

I bring a corporate past, a trauma-informed practice and a deep grounding in functional movement and breath. I speak your team's language — and then I help them put it down for a while, and feel something shift.

Inside a session

Practical tools they can use at their desk.

A typical session moves systematically from grounding to breath to gentle movement and into deep rest — prioritising internal awareness over performance, so no one feels they have to "be good at yoga."

  • Mindful awareness & grounding — attention to breath, body and the present moment.
  • Breathwork — box breathing and Brahmari (humming-bee breath) to settle the nervous system.
  • Vagal toning — simple techniques like ear massage and slow breathing to stimulate the vagus nerve.
  • Gentle movement — flowing sun-salutation sequences to build warmth and focus.
  • Shavasana — final rest to let the body and nervous system integrate.
Techniques they can use anywhere — after a stressful drive, or right at their desk.
